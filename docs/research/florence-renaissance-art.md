## üèõÔ∏è Major Attractions

### 1. Uffizi Gallery (Gallerie degli Uffizi)

The world's greatest collection of Renaissance art, housed in a 16th-century building designed by Vasari.

**Booking Strategy:**
- **Book on the official site:** uffizi.it/en/tickets ‚Äî mandatory reservation
- **Book 2-4 weeks ahead** in summer; tickets sell out
- **Ticket price:** ‚Ç¨25 + ‚Ç¨4 booking fee (2025/2026 pricing)
- **Skip-the-line:** All online tickets are timed-entry and use Entrance 3 (priority entrance)
- **Best time slot:** First thing in the morning (8:15 AM opening) or late afternoon (after 4 PM). Midday is peak chaos
- **Duration:** Plan 3-4 hours minimum; could spend all day

**Must-See Works:**
- **Botticelli:** *The Birth of Venus* (Room 10-14) and *Primavera* ‚Äî the crown jewels
- **Leonardo da Vinci:** *Annunciation* and *Adoration of the Magi* (unfinished)
- **Michelangelo:** *Doni Tondo* (Holy Family) ‚Äî his only finished panel painting
- **Raphael:** *Madonna of the Goldfinch*
- **Caravaggio:** *Medusa* and *Sacrifice of Isaac*
- **Titian:** *Venus of Urbino*
- **Gentile da Fabriano:** *Adoration of the Magi* (International Gothic masterpiece)
- **Cimabue & Giotto:** Room 2 ‚Äî see the birth of Renaissance painting in three altar pieces side by side

**Pro Tips:**
- Don't try to see everything ‚Äî pick your must-sees and beeline for them
- The caf√© terrace on the top floor has a stunning view of Palazzo Vecchio
- Tuesday is the busiest day (museums closed Monday, so Tuesday gets the overflow)
- Combined Uffizi + Palazzo Pitti ticket available (‚Ç¨38)

### 2. Galleria dell'Accademia (Michelangelo's David)

**The main event:** Michelangelo's *David* (1501-1504), 17 feet of Carrara marble perfection.

**Booking:**
- **Official site:** galleriaaccademiafirenze.it
- **Ticket:** ‚Ç¨20 + ‚Ç¨4 booking fee
- **Reserve in advance** ‚Äî this sells out faster than the Uffizi in summer
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours (it's a smaller museum)

**What else to see:**
- Michelangelo's *Prisoners/Slaves* ‚Äî four unfinished sculptures lining the hallway to David. Figures seemingly struggling to emerge from raw marble. Hauntingly beautiful
- *St. Matthew* (unfinished) by Michelangelo
- The Musical Instruments collection
- Giambologna's plaster model of *Rape of the Sabines*

**Pro Tips:**
- Go at opening (8:15 AM) ‚Äî the gallery is small and gets packed fast
- The David is at the end of the main corridor ‚Äî you'll see the Prisoners first (they're incredible, don't rush past)
- Takes about 45 minutes if you're focused, 1.5 hours to savor

### 3. Duomo Complex (Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore)

Florence's iconic cathedral with Brunelleschi's revolutionary dome ‚Äî the largest masonry dome ever built.

**Components:**
1. **The Cathedral** ‚Äî Free entry, no ticket needed (but lines can be long)
2. **Brunelleschi's Cupola (Dome Climb)** ‚Äî 463 steps, the highlight
3. **Giotto's Bell Tower (Campanile)** ‚Äî 414 steps, arguably better views
4. **Baptistery of San Giovanni** ‚Äî Ghiberti's "Gates of Paradise" bronze doors
5. **Museo dell'Opera del Duomo** ‚Äî Original doors, Michelangelo's late *Piet√†*
6. **Santa Reparata Crypt** ‚Äî Beneath the cathedral

**Dome Climb Booking:**
- **Official site:** duomo.firenze.it
- **Combined ticket (Brunelleschi Pass):** Covers dome + bell tower + baptistery + museum + crypt (~‚Ç¨30)
- **Dome climb requires timed reservation** ‚Äî book ASAP, limited slots
- **New rules from March 2025:** Stricter entry rules; reservation is mandatory, no walk-ups
- **463 narrow steps**, no elevator ‚Äî not for claustrophobia or mobility issues
- At the top: 360¬∞ panorama of Florence and surrounding Tuscan hills
- Inside: Vasari & Zuccari's *Last Judgment* fresco up close

**Pro Tips:**
- Climb the Bell Tower instead of (or in addition to) the dome ‚Äî shorter line, and you get the dome IN your photos
- Morning is best for the dome climb (less heat, better light)
- The Opera del Duomo Museum is excellent and often uncrowded ‚Äî don't skip it

### 4. Ponte Vecchio

Medieval stone bridge lined with jewelry shops (originally butcher shops until the Medici complained about the smell).

- **Free to walk across** anytime
- Best photographed from Ponte Santa Trinita or the Uffizi's riverside windows
- The Vasari Corridor runs above the shops (private passageway connecting Palazzo Vecchio to Palazzo Pitti) ‚Äî occasionally open for tours
- Sunset views from adjacent bridges are gorgeous
- **Warning:** Extremely crowded midday. Visit early morning or at dusk

---

### 5. Piazza della Signoria

Florence's political heart since the 14th century. Open-air sculpture gallery.

**Highlights:**
- **Palazzo Vecchio** ‚Äî Town hall since 1299, climbable tower with views (‚Ç¨18 ticket)
- **Loggia dei Lanzi** ‚Äî Free open-air sculpture gallery featuring:
  - Cellini's *Perseus with the Head of Medusa* (bronze masterpiece)
  - Giambologna's *Rape of the Sabines*
  - Ancient Roman sculptures
- **Neptune Fountain** (Ammannati) ‚Äî locals call it "Il Biancone" (the big white one)
- **Copy of David** stands where the original once stood (until 1873)
- The spot where Savonarola was burned at the stake (1498) ‚Äî marked by a plaque

### 6. San Lorenzo Market & Medici Chapels

**San Lorenzo Market:**
- Outdoor leather market surrounding the Basilica di San Lorenzo
- Great for leather goods, souvenirs, scarves ‚Äî but **haggle!** Prices are inflated for tourists
- Indoor Mercato Centrale (see Food section below)

**Medici Chapels (Cappelle Medicee):**
- **Ticket:** ‚Ç¨11 + ‚Ç¨3 booking fee
- **New Sacristy** by Michelangelo ‚Äî his architectural masterpiece with tomb sculptures:
  - *Dawn* and *Dusk* (Lorenzo de' Medici's tomb)
  - *Day* and *Night* (Giuliano de' Medici's tomb)
- **Chapel of the Princes** ‚Äî overwhelming Baroque chapel covered in semi-precious stones
- Duration: ~45 minutes

**Basilica di San Lorenzo:**
- Brunelleschi's first church design
- Donatello's bronze pulpits (his last works)
- Laurentian Library by Michelangelo (stunning staircase)

### 7. Piazzale Michelangelo ‚Äî Sunset

**The** sunset spot in Florence. Panoramic terrace on the south bank hilltop.

- **Free** ‚Äî always open
- Walk up from Ponte Vecchio area (~20 min uphill) or take Bus 12 or 13 from the station
- Bronze copy of David at the center
- Arrive 30-45 minutes before sunset to claim a good spot (bring wine!)
- The view: all of Florence spread below ‚Äî Duomo, Palazzo Vecchio, Ponte Vecchio, the Arno
- Continue uphill to **San Miniato al Monte** ‚Äî a stunning Romanesque church (11th century) that's far less crowded and equally beautiful at sunset

---

### 8. Mercato Centrale (Central Market Food Hall)

Two-level food paradise inside a 19th-century iron-and-glass structure.

**Ground floor:** Traditional market ‚Äî fresh produce, meat, cheese, truffles, dried goods. Open mornings (7 AM - 2 PM)
**Upper floor:** Modern food hall with artisan food stalls. Open 10 AM - midnight daily

**Must-try stalls:**
- **Da Nerbone** (ground floor, since 1872) ‚Äî *lampredotto* (tripe sandwich) and *bollito* (boiled beef). Iconic Florentine street food
- **L'Antico Trippaio** ‚Äî More lampredotto (you need to try it)
- Fresh pasta stalls, pizza, gelato, wine bars upstairs
- Truffle products ‚Äî Florence is truffle country

## ‚òÄÔ∏è Summer Crowd Management Strategies

Florence in summer (June-August) is HOT (35¬∞C+) and PACKED. Here's how to survive:

### Timing Strategy
1. **Museums at opening** (8:15 AM) ‚Äî be in line 15 min before. Uffizi and Accademia are manageable at opening
2. **Midday siesta** (12-3 PM) ‚Äî retreat to your hotel, a caf√©, or air-conditioned Mercato Centrale. The heat and crowds peak simultaneously
3. **Late afternoon** (4-7 PM) ‚Äî second museum window. Many museums are open until 6:30 PM
4. **Sunset** at Piazzale Michelangelo (8:30-9 PM in summer)
5. **Evening passeggiata** ‚Äî walk the city after dark when it's cooler and magical

### Booking Strategy
- **Book everything 2-4 weeks ahead** ‚Äî Uffizi, Accademia, Dome climb all sell out
- **Tuesday & weekends** are worst. **Wednesday-Thursday** tend to be slightly less crowded
- **First week of August** can actually be less crowded (Italians leave for their own vacations, Ferragosto)

### Beat the Heat
- Carry water ‚Äî Florence has public drinking fountains (fontanelle)
- Wear a hat, sunscreen, comfortable shoes (cobblestones everywhere)
- Gelato breaks are mandatory (this is science)
- Boboli Gardens offer shade but are large and hilly ‚Äî save energy
- Churches are free air conditioning (and free art)

### Lesser-Known Alternatives When Crowds Overwhelm
- **Museo di San Marco** ‚Äî Fra Angelico frescoes, rarely crowded, stunning
- **Brancacci Chapel** (Oltrarno) ‚Äî Masaccio frescoes that started the Renaissance
- **Museo Bargello** ‚Äî Donatello's David, Verrocchio, uncrowded
- **Palazzo Strozzi** ‚Äî Excellent rotating exhibitions
- **San Miniato al Monte** ‚Äî Stunning hilltop church, tourist-free
