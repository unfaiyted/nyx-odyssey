## üèá IL PALIO DI SIENA ‚Äî July 2, 2026

### ‚ö° THIS IS THE BIG ONE. WE'RE IN ITALY FOR THIS.

The Palio is a bareback horse race around Piazza del Campo that has been run since the medieval era. It happens twice yearly: **July 2** (Palio di Provenzano, in honor of the Madonna of Provenzano) and August 16. The race itself lasts roughly **90 seconds** ‚Äî three laps of the shell-shaped piazza ‚Äî but the entire spectacle spans **4 days** of trials, parades, rituals, and neighborhood rivalry that makes the race almost secondary to the experience.

### How It Works

- **17 contrade** (neighborhoods) exist, but only **10 race** each Palio. Seven that didn't race the previous corresponding Palio are automatic; three more are drawn by lot
- Horses are assigned to contrade by lottery (the *tratta*) ‚Äî contrade have their own jockey but NOT their own horse
- Jockeys receive a *nerbo* (bullwhip) to urge their horse or hinder opponents ‚Äî this is medieval, not sanitized
- A horse can win **without its jockey** (called a *cavallo scosso*) ‚Äî it just has to cross the finish line first
- The *mossa* (start) is notoriously chaotic ‚Äî horses line up between two ropes, with the 10th contrada starting from behind and choosing the moment to charge

### üìÖ Schedule of Events (June 29 ‚Äì July 2)

**June 29 ‚Äî Tratta (Horse Assignment)**
- Morning: Horses are selected and assigned to contrade by lottery
- Afternoon trial (19:30): First trial run

**June 30 ‚Äî Trial Runs**
- Morning trial: 9:00 AM
- Afternoon trial: 19:30

**July 1 ‚Äî General Trial + Contrada Dinners**
- Morning trial: 9:00 AM
- Afternoon: **Prova Generale** (dress rehearsal, 19:30) ‚Äî the most important trial
- Evening: **Grand propitiatory dinners** in each contrada ‚Äî THIS IS INCREDIBLE (see below)

**July 2 ‚Äî PALIO DAY**
- 8:00 AM: *Messa del Fantino* (Jockey's Mass) at Palazzo Pubblico chapel
- 9:00 AM: Final trial (*Provaccia*) ‚Äî intentionally light
- 10:30 AM: *Segnatura dei fantini* ‚Äî jockeys officially confirmed, no substitutions after this
- ~15:00: Horses blessed in each contrada's oratory ‚Äî **deeply moving ritual, worth seeing**
- 15:30: First warning signal
- 16:40: Track clearing begins
- 17:00-17:15: Historical Parade (*Corteo Storico*) enters Piazza del Campo ‚Äî **spectacular medieval pageantry** with flag-throwers, drummers, figurants in full Renaissance regalia
- 19:30: **THE RACE** ‚Äî horses exit the Podest√† Courtyard, the *mossa* begins
- Post-race: Winning contrada **EXPLODES** in celebration ‚Äî partying lasts until dawn

### üéüÔ∏è Do You Need Tickets?

**For the center of the piazza (the free zone): NO TICKETS NEEDED ‚Äî it's FREE**

This is the authentic experience ‚Äî standing in the packed center of the Campo with 30,000-40,000 people. It's raw, intense, and electric. However:

**Viewing Options:**

1. **Free standing in the piazza center** (the *curva*)
   - FREE but arrive **by noon at latest** (many say by 10-11 AM)
   - Once you're in, you're IN ‚Äî no leaving and re-entering
   - You'll be standing for 6-8+ hours in July heat
   - Views are limited (you'll see horses fly past, but won't see the whole track)
   - The communal energy is absolutely unforgettable ‚Äî this IS the Palio
   - Bring: water (no lids allowed on bottles), snacks/energy bars, sunscreen, hat
   - NO chairs, stools, or strollers allowed

2. **Balcony/window seats (grandinate)**
   - ‚Ç¨150-400+ per person depending on location
   - Booked through building residents or local agencies
   - Include shade, views of the entire track, and usually toilet access
   - **Book 3-6 months ahead** ‚Äî these sell out fast
   - Check: paliodisiena.com, sienaincoming.it, or contact hotels for connections

3. **Elevated free spot near Fonte Gaia**
   - The slope near the fountain offers slightly elevated views
   - Arrive by 2 PM to claim a spot
   - A good middle ground ‚Äî better sightlines than deep in the crowd

4. **Trial runs (prove)** ‚Äî Much less crowded, still thrilling
   - Great way to experience the piazza atmosphere without the extreme crush
   - Enter by 18:00 for evening trials

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety & Practical Tips

- **No children under 10** allowed in the piazza on race day (even with adults)
- **No dogs** allowed
- **Wear closed-toe shoes** ‚Äî you will get stepped on in the crowd
- **No containers with lids** or frozen bottles allowed (security measure)
- The crowd is intense but generally safe ‚Äî maintain situational awareness
- **Post-race celebration gets rowdy** ‚Äî winning contrada parties hard, stay alert for flying flags and surging crowds
- The Palio involves **real danger for riders** ‚Äî this is not a sanitized tourist event. Falls at San Martino curve are common
- **Most restaurants close** during the race ‚Äî eat beforehand or pack food
- **Hydrate aggressively** ‚Äî July in Tuscany is HOT (30-35¬∞C / 86-95¬∞F)

### üçΩÔ∏è Contrada Dinners (Cene della Prova Generale)

The evening of July 1 (after the General Trial), each contrada hosts a **massive outdoor banquet** in their neighborhood streets. Long tables, hundreds of people, local food, flowing wine, singing contrada songs. This is the SOUL of the Palio.

- **Can tourists attend?** YES ‚Äî many contrade sell seats to outsiders
- **How to book:** Contact the contrada directly (each has a website/social media) or ask your hotel. Reserve **weeks in advance**
- **Cost:** Usually ‚Ç¨30-50 per person for a full multi-course meal with wine
- **What to expect:** Incredible atmosphere, communal dining, contrada songs and chants, genuine local hospitality
- **Tip:** Buy a *fazzoletto* (contrada scarf, ~‚Ç¨15) from their museum and wear it proudly

## üèõÔ∏è Major Sights

### 1. Piazza del Campo

The shell-shaped central square ‚Äî one of the most beautiful public spaces in the world. Even without the Palio, it's mesmerizing.

- **Shape:** Unique concave fan divided into 9 segments (representing the medieval Council of Nine)
- **Torre del Mangia:** The 102-meter bell tower of the Palazzo Pubblico ‚Äî climb it for panoramic views (400 steps, ‚Ç¨10). Open 10:00-19:00 in summer
- **Palazzo Pubblico & Civic Museum:** Houses Ambrogio Lorenzetti's famous *Allegory of Good and Bad Government* frescoes (1338) ‚Äî one of the most important secular artworks of the Middle Ages. ‚Ç¨10 entry
- **Fonte Gaia:** The ornate marble fountain at the top of the piazza (replica ‚Äî originals by Jacopo della Quercia are in the museum)
- **Best way to enjoy it:** Grab a gelato, sit on the brick campo, and people-watch. The piazza slopes downward toward the Palazzo ‚Äî pick a spot and soak it in

### 2. Siena Cathedral (Duomo di Siena)

A masterpiece of Italian Romanesque-Gothic architecture. The striking black-and-white striped marble exterior is iconic.

- **Interior highlights:**
  - **Marble floor:** 56 incredibly detailed narrative scenes carved and inlaid over centuries (partially covered; fully uncovered Aug-Oct). Extraordinary
  - **Piccolomini Library:** Breathtaking frescoes by Pinturicchio depicting the life of Pope Pius II. Don't skip this ‚Äî it's a hidden gem inside the cathedral
  - **Pulpit by Nicola Pisano** (1268): One of the great masterpieces of Gothic sculpture
  - **Bernini's sculptures** in the Chigi Chapel
- **Battistero (Baptistery):** Below the cathedral, with a stunning baptismal font featuring panels by Donatello, Ghiberti, and Jacopo della Quercia
- **OPA SI Pass:** Combined ticket (~‚Ç¨15-20) covers Cathedral, Piccolomini Library, Baptistery, Crypt, and the Panoramic Terrace (Facciatone) ‚Äî the unfinished wall of the planned cathedral expansion, offering spectacular views
- **The "New Cathedral":** Siena once tried to build a cathedral so massive that the current Duomo would be just the transept. The 1348 Black Death killed so many that they never finished ‚Äî the remaining wall (Facciatone) is now a viewing platform. Haunting and beautiful

### 3. The Contrade (Medieval Neighborhoods)

Siena is divided into **17 contrade**, each with its own:
- Church/oratory, museum, fountain, flag, and animal symbol
- Annual festivals and feast days
- Deep rivalries (some contrade are *alleati*, some are bitter enemies)
- Baptism ceremonies (babies are baptized into the contrada, not just the church)
- Social clubs, community activities, charitable works

**Notable Contrade to Explore:**
- **Oca (Goose):** Near the Duomo ‚Äî strong, historic, and proud
- **Torre (Tower):** Enemy of Oca ‚Äî the rivalry is legendary
- **Selva (Forest):** Known for the beautiful Rhino fountain
- **Aquila (Eagle):** The only contrada with territory outside the city walls
- **Lupa (She-Wolf):** Near San Domenico, with Roman mythology connections

**How to explore:** Wander the streets and look for contrada flags, street lamps with animal symbols, and small museums. Many contrade have museums open to visitors (‚Ç¨5-8) with Palio memorabilia, historic banners (*drappelloni*), and costumes

### 4. Other Notable Sights

- **Basilica of San Domenico:** Houses the preserved head of St. Catherine of Siena (yes, really). Important Gothic church
- **Santa Maria della Scala:** Former medieval hospital, now a massive museum complex with frescoes, archaeological exhibits, and underground tunnels
- **Via Banchi di Sopra / Via di Citt√†:** The main shopping/strolling streets ‚Äî beautiful palazzi on every side
- **Fortezza Medicea:** The Medici fortress, now a park with wine bars and panoramic views. Home to the *Enoteca Italiana*

## üç∑ Wine Bars & Chianti

Siena sits at the heart of **Chianti Classico** country. The wines here are world-class.

### Wine Bars in Siena

1. **Enoteca Italiana** (Fortezza Medicea)
   - Inside the Medici fortress ‚Äî Italy's national wine library
   - Vast selection of Italian wines by the glass
   - Beautiful setting with terrace views
   - Good for a pre-dinner aperitivo

2. **Wine Cellar by Sapordivino**
   - Top-rated wine bar in Siena
   - Knowledgeable staff, excellent Tuscan selection
   - Perfect for learning about Brunello vs Chianti Classico

3. **Compagnia dei Vinattieri** (doubles as restaurant ‚Äî see above)
   - Deep Tuscan wine list ‚Äî great for pairing with dinner

4. **Rivella Enoteca**
   - Intimate, local-feeling wine bar
   - Good cheese and salumi boards with wine

### Key Wines to Know
- **Chianti Classico DOCG:** The real deal ‚Äî from vineyards between Florence and Siena. Look for the Black Rooster (*Gallo Nero*) label. Sangiovese-based, medium body, perfect with pasta and grilled meats
- **Brunello di Montalcino DOCG:** Big, prestigious, age-worthy. 100% Sangiovese, from Montalcino (~40 min south). Expensive but extraordinary
- **Rosso di Montalcino:** Brunello's younger, more affordable sibling ‚Äî excellent value
- **Vino Nobile di Montepulciano DOCG:** Another great Sangiovese from nearby Montepulciano
- **Vernaccia di San Gimignano DOCG:** The region's star white ‚Äî crisp, refreshing. Perfect with seafood or aperitivo
- **Vin Santo:** Sweet dessert wine, amber-colored, served with cantucci

## üöó Tuscan Countryside Day Drives

### Drive 1: Chianti Wine Route (Half Day)

**Route:** Siena ‚Üí Castellina in Chianti ‚Üí Radda in Chianti ‚Üí Greve in Chianti (~60 km)

- Wind through rolling vineyards and olive groves on the **SS222 (Chiantigiana)**
- Stop at **Castello di Brolio** ‚Äî the birthplace of Chianti Classico, stunning castle with wine tasting and panoramic views
- **Radda in Chianti:** Tiny hilltop village, stop at **Enoteca Wine Bar Casaporciatti** for a glass
- **Greve in Chianti:** Charming main piazza, famous **Macelleria Falorni** (butcher since 1729, tasting available)
- Many wineries offer tastings ‚Äî book ahead in summer. Try: Fonterutoli, Castello di Ama, Rocca delle Mac√¨e

### Drive 2: San Gimignano (Half Day ‚Äî HIGHLY RECOMMENDED COMBO)

**Distance from Siena:** ~45 minutes (40 km)

San Gimignano is the **"Medieval Manhattan"** ‚Äî famous for its 14 surviving medieval towers (originally 72!). Absolutely worth combining with Siena.

- **Getting there:** Easy drive, park at P1 or P2 (‚Ç¨2/hour). Or take the Tiemme bus from Siena (~75 min with connection in Poggibonsi)
- **Piazza della Cisterna:** The beautiful triangular main square ‚Äî gelato at **Gelateria Dondoli** (multiple world gelato champion, no joke)
- **Torre Grossa:** Climb the tallest tower for incredible 360¬∞ Tuscan views (‚Ç¨9 with Civic Museum)
- **Collegiata:** The main church, covered floor-to-ceiling in extraordinary 14th-century frescoes ‚Äî Old Testament on left, New Testament on right
- **Vernaccia wine:** San Gimignano's signature white ‚Äî taste it everywhere. Visit **Fattoria Poggio Alloro** for wine + saffron tasting
- **Tip:** Visit early morning or late afternoon ‚Äî midday is cruise-ship tour group territory
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours for the town itself, more with wine tasting

### Drive 3: Val d'Orcia (Full Day)

**Route:** Siena ‚Üí Montalcino ‚Üí Sant'Antimo Abbey ‚Üí Pienza ‚Üí Montepulciano (~120 km loop)

- **Val d'Orcia** is a UNESCO World Heritage landscape ‚Äî the iconic "Tuscany" of postcards (cypress-lined roads, golden hills, medieval hill towns)
- **Montalcino:** Home of Brunello ‚Äî visit **Enoteca La Fortezza** inside the medieval fortress for tastings with a view
- **Sant'Antimo Abbey:** Romanesque beauty in a cypress grove ‚Äî if you're lucky, catch the monks singing Gregorian chant
- **Pienza:** The "ideal city" redesigned by Pope Pius II ‚Äî gorgeous piazza and the BEST **pecorino cheese** in Italy. Buy from shops on the main street
- **Montepulciano:** Another stunning hill town ‚Äî *Vino Nobile* tastings in underground wine cellars. Visit **Cantina De' Ricci** or **Contucci** for free cellar tastings
- **Iconic photo spots:** The cypress-lined road near Monticchiello, the lone chapel of Vitaleta, the rolling hills near San Quirico d'Orcia

## üìã Practical Information

### When to Visit
- **July 1-3** for the Palio (arrive July 1 to catch the General Trial and contrada dinners)
- If possible, come a day earlier (June 30) to settle in and watch a morning trial
- July is HOT ‚Äî 30-35¬∞C. Bring sun protection, stay hydrated

### Where to Stay (During Palio)
- **Book 6+ months ahead** ‚Äî 85% of central Siena sells out by January for Palio week
- Hotels in the center charge premium (‚Ç¨300-500+/night during Palio)
- **Better value:** Boutique guesthouses in quieter contrade (Bruco, Selva), or agriturismos just outside the walls
- Some contrade rent apartments with coveted balcony access ‚Äî ask locally
- **Budget option:** Stay in San Gimignano or Chianti and drive in for race day (but you'll miss the evening atmosphere)

### Getting Around Siena
- **Walk everything** ‚Äî the centro storico is compact (~25 min end to end)
- Streets are steep and medieval ‚Äî wear good shoes
- The city is roughly divided into three *terzi* (thirds): Citt√†, San Martino, and Camollia

### Money & Tips
- Budget ~‚Ç¨80-120/day for food and sightseeing (excluding accommodation)
- Palio balcony seats: ‚Ç¨150-400 if desired
- Contrada dinner: ‚Ç¨30-50
- Combined cathedral pass: ‚Ç¨15-20
- Wine tastings in Chianti: ‚Ç¨15-30 per winery
