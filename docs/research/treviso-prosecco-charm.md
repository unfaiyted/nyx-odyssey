## üè∞ Treviso Old Town ‚Äî The Highlights

### Piazza dei Signori
- The main square and "living room" of Treviso
- Framed by the 13th-century **Palazzo dei Trecento** (council hall, still in use)
- Outdoor caf√©s under arcades ‚Äî perfect for a morning espresso
- Featured in the Italian film *Signore e Signori*

### The Canals ‚Äî "Little Venice"
- **Buranelli Canal:** The iconic postcard shot ‚Äî ivy-draped balconies, pastel houses reflected in the water. Named after merchants from Burano who traded here
- **Cagnan Canal (Cagnan Grande):** Flows through the heart of the old town, powers medieval water wheels still turning today
- **Riviera Garibaldi:** Scenic canal-side walk with weeping willows
- Much smaller and more intimate than Venice ‚Äî think "Venice's quieter, prettier cousin"

### Isola della Pescheria (Fish Market Island)
- A tiny island in the Cagnan canal that hosts Treviso's **fish market**
- Open **Tuesday‚ÄìSaturday mornings** (best before 11 AM)
- Surrounded by fruit/vegetable stalls on the adjacent canal banks
- One of the most atmospheric market settings in the Veneto
- **Don't miss:** The views from the bridges on either side

### The Duomo (Cathedral)
- Neoclassical fa√ßade hides interior treasures
- **Titian's Annunciation** in the Malchiostro Chapel
- Chapels by Tullio Lombardo
- Free entry

### San Nicol√≤ Church
- Gothic gem with remarkable **frescoes by Tommaso da Modena** (1352)
- Famous for the **earliest known painting of eyeglasses** ‚Äî a monk reading with spectacles, centuries before they were common
- Adjacent Chapter House has 40 Dominican scholars at their desks, each with distinct personality
- Free entry, often overlooked by tourists

### Fontana delle Tette ("Fountain of the Breasts")
- Hidden in a courtyard off Calle del Podest√†
- Under the Venetian Republic, it dispensed **red and white wine** during festivals
- Current version is a replica (original damaged in WWII)
- Quirky photo op

### The City Walls
- 500-year-old Venetian-era defensive walls still encircle the old town
- **Porta San Tomaso** ‚Äî grand entrance gate with the Lion of St. Mark
- **Bastione San Paolo** ‚Äî best viewpoint
- Nice for a short stroll along the perimeter

## üç∑ Prosecco Wine Region

### The Basics
- Treviso is the gateway to the **Conegliano-Valdobbiadene Prosecco Superiore DOCG** zone
- **Valdobbiadene** is ~45 min north of Treviso by car (no practical public transit)
- The hills were designated a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** in 2019
- **Cartizze** is the grand cru ‚Äî a tiny 107-hectare hilltop producing the finest Prosecco

### Winery Tours & Tastings

#### Self-Drive Route (Recommended)
The **Strada del Prosecco e Vini dei Colli Conegliano Valdobbiadene** is a scenic wine road winding through the hills:
- Drive from Treviso north through Conegliano ‚Üí Valdobbiadene
- Rolling hills covered in vineyards, stunning photo ops
- Many wineries welcome walk-ins, but booking ahead is better

## üçù Food & Drink in Treviso

### Signature Dishes

#### Tiramisu ‚Äî Born Here
Treviso claims to be the **birthplace of tiramisu**. Two restaurants dispute the title:
- **Le Beccherie** ‚Äî Most widely credited, claims invention in the 1960s. Classic version with espresso-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone
- **Toni del Spin** ‚Äî Also claims the invention. Traditional trattoria with excellent version

**Verdict:** Try both and decide for yourself.

#### Radicchio di Treviso
- **Season:** Late autumn through winter (November‚ÄìFebruary)
- Two varieties: **Precoce** (early, wider leaves) and **Tardivo** (late, prized elongated form ‚Äî the real deal)
- **Tardivo** is the star ‚Äî sweet-bitter, crunchy, looks like a purple flame
- Preparation: Grilled with olive oil, in risotto, or raw in salads
- If visiting Nov-Feb, this is a MUST ‚Äî it's a protected IGP product, tastes nothing like the sad radicchio in US supermarkets
- **Radicchio festivals** happen in January in surrounding towns (Castelfranco, Casier)

## üëî Benetton ‚Äî Fashion History

### The Benetton Connection
- **United Colors of Benetton** was founded in Treviso in **1965** by the Benetton family
- **Headquarters:** Still based in Ponzano Veneto, just outside Treviso (~5 km)
- The company revolutionized fast fashion with bold colors and controversial **Oliviero Toscani** advertising campaigns (the "United Colors" multicultural imagery)
- At its peak in the 1980s-90s, Benetton was one of the world's most recognized brands
- The family also owns **Autogrill** (highway rest stops) and **Atlantia** (Italian toll roads)

### What to See
- **F√°brica** ‚Äî Benetton's research and communication center in a restored 17th-century villa in Treviso. Occasionally hosts exhibitions and cultural events
- **Imago Mundi** ‚Äî Benetton's art collection project, sometimes exhibited at F√°brica
- No formal "Benetton museum," but the company's presence is felt throughout the city
- The Benetton rugby team (**Benetton Rugby Treviso**) plays in Treviso ‚Äî check match schedules if interested

### Verdict
**Treviso is a strong half-day destination** that becomes a **standout full-day trip when combined with the Prosecco Hills**. On its own, the old town is charming but compact ‚Äî 3-4 hours covers the highlights. The real magic is the combo:

- Morning: Treviso's canals, markets, cicchetti
- Afternoon: Rolling through UNESCO Prosecco vineyards, wine tasting

**If choosing between Treviso and Venice:** Venice is the once-in-a-lifetime must-do. Treviso is the "I want the real Italy" alternative. Both are worth doing if time permits.

**If you only have one free day:** Venice or the Dolomites probably win. But if you've already done Venice, Treviso + Prosecco is a perfect "relaxed Italian day."

**Best season:** Late autumn (Nov-Dec) for Radicchio di Treviso Tardivo season. Spring/early summer for the vineyards at their greenest. Summer can be hot and humid.
