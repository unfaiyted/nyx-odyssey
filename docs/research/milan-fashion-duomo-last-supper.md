## ‚õ™ Duomo di Milano

Milan's iconic Gothic cathedral ‚Äî the largest church in Italy and 3rd largest in the world.

### Key Info
- **Address:** Piazza del Duomo, 20122 Milano
- **Hours:** Cathedral 9:00‚Äì19:00 daily (last entry 18:10). Rooftop hours vary.
- **Website:** [duomomilano.it](https://www.duomomilano.it/en/)

### Tickets & Access
- **Cathedral only:** ~‚Ç¨5
- **Duomo Pass (Cathedral + Rooftop by stairs + Museum + Archaeological Area):** ~‚Ç¨15
- **Duomo Pass + Lift (elevator to rooftop):** ~‚Ç¨20
- **Book online** at [duomomilano.it](https://www.duomomilano.it/en/) to skip ticket lines
- **Rooftop by stairs:** 250 steps, but absolutely worth it. The elevator is for those who can't manage stairs.
- **Tip:** Go to the rooftop at golden hour (1-2 hours before closing) for incredible light on the marble spires and city views. On clear days you can see the Alps.

### What to See
- **Interior:** Massive nave, stunning stained glass (largest collection in the world), the nail from the True Cross displayed high above the apse
- **Rooftop terraces:** Walk among 135 marble spires, 3,400+ statues. The Madonnina (golden Madonna statue) at the peak. Unreal photo opportunities.
- **Archaeological area:** Roman baptistry ruins beneath the Duomo (4th century)
- **Duomo Museum:** Adjacent, houses original sculptures and artifacts

### Pro Tips
- Dress code enforced: covered shoulders and knees required (they provide paper coverups but it's embarrassing)
- Morning (9-10 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM) for fewer crowds
- Allow 2-3 hours total (cathedral + rooftop + piazza)

## üèõÔ∏è Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

Italy's oldest active shopping gallery (1877), connecting Piazza del Duomo to Piazza della Scala.

### Why Visit (Even If Not Shopping)
- **Architecture:** Stunning iron-and-glass vaulted ceiling, ornate mosaics, neo-Renaissance design. One of the world's most beautiful covered passages.
- **The Bull Mosaic:** Find the Savoy coat of arms with a bull on the floor. Tradition says spinning your heel on the bull's... private parts brings good luck. Tourists queue for this.
- **Luxury brands:** Prada (flagship, born here in 1913), Louis Vuitton, Gucci, Versace, Armani ‚Äî window shopping is free
- **Historic cafes:** Caff√® Camparino (1867, original Campari bar ‚Äî try a Campari Spritz at the source) and Caff√® Biffi
- **Free to enter** ‚Äî it's a public arcade

### Practical
- **Location:** Directly adjacent to Piazza del Duomo. You'll walk through it naturally.
- **Time needed:** 15-30 min to walk through, longer if you stop for a drink
- **Best time:** Evening when it's beautifully lit

## üç∑ Navigli Canal District ‚Äî Evening Drinks & Aperitivo

Milan's most atmospheric neighborhood for nightlife, built along the historic canal system (designed partly by Leonardo da Vinci).

### The Aperitivo Tradition (Milanese Style)
- **What:** Pre-dinner drinks (typically 6-9 PM) where your ‚Ç¨8-12 cocktail comes with generous free food ‚Äî not just peanuts, but full buffets of pasta, bruschetta, salads, rice, meats
- **Origin:** Milan invented modern aperitivo culture. It's practically a religion here.
- **The drink:** Negroni, Negroni Sbagliato (born in Milan!), Aperol Spritz, Campari Soda, or a classic Americano cocktail

### Best Spots Along the Navigli
- **Naviglio Grande:** The main canal ‚Äî most bars and restaurants line this waterway
- **Naviglio Pavese:** Quieter, more local
- **Darsena:** The basin where canals meet, renovated with a modern waterfront

### Recommended Bars & Restaurants
- **Mag Caff√®** ‚Äî Craft cocktails in a tiny canal-side space. Intimate, creative drinks.
- **Rita** ‚Äî Great aperitivo buffet, lively vibe, right on Naviglio Grande
- **Ugo** ‚Äî Hip wine bar, excellent natural wine selection
- **Fonderie Milanesi** ‚Äî Set in a converted foundry, great cocktails and atmosphere
- **Langosteria Bistrot** ‚Äî High-end seafood if splurging
- **Al Pont de Ferr** ‚Äî 1 Michelin star, canal-side fine dining (book well ahead)

### Practical
- **Getting there:** Metro M2 to Porta Genova, then 5-min walk
- **When:** Aperitivo starts around 6-7 PM. Bars fill up by 8 PM on weekends. The area stays lively until late.
- **Market:** Antique market along Naviglio Grande on last Sunday of each month (Mercatone dell'Antiquariato) ‚Äî great for vintage finds
- **Tip:** Walk the full length of Naviglio Grande for the best atmosphere. The further from the Metro, the more local it gets.

## üé® Brera District & Pinacoteca di Brera

- **Hours:** Tues‚ÄìSun 8:30‚Äì19:15 (closed Monday)
- **Cost:** ~‚Ç¨15

- **Food:** Excellent aperitivo spots here too ‚Äî more refined than Navigli, less touristy

## üèóÔ∏è Modern Architecture

### Bosco Verticale (Vertical Forest)
- **What:** Two residential towers covered with 900+ trees and 20,000+ plants. Won the International Highrise Award (2014). Architect: Stefano Boeri.
- **Location:** Via Gaetano de Castillia 11, Porta Nuova district
- **Can you enter?** No ‚Äî private residences. But the ground-level piazza and surrounding park are public. Best viewed and photographed from Piazza Gae Aulenti.
- **Nearby:** BAM (Biblioteca degli Alberi Milano) ‚Äî a beautiful modern park at the base. Great for a stroll.

### CityLife District
- **What:** Milan's newest skyline ‚Äî three iconic skyscrapers by international starchitects:
  - **The Straight One (Torre Isozaki):** Arata Isozaki, 209m ‚Äî Milan's tallest
  - **The Twisted One (Torre Hadid):** Zaha Hadid, 170m ‚Äî twisting design
  - **The Curved One (Torre Libeskind):** Daniel Libeskind, 175m
- **CityLife Shopping District:** Modern mall at the base
- **CityLife Park:** Large public park, great for walking
- **Getting there:** Metro M5 Tre Torri
- **Worth it?** If you love architecture, yes. Otherwise, a quick look from the park is sufficient.

### Fondazione Prada
- **What:** Contemporary art museum in a former gin distillery, expanded by Rem Koolhaas/OMA. Stunning architecture.
- **Bar Luce:** Wes Anderson-designed caf√© inside ‚Äî worth a coffee just for the aesthetic
- **Hours:** Wed‚ÄìMon 10:00‚Äì19:00 (closed Tues)
- **Cost:** ~‚Ç¨15
- **Location:** Largo Isarco 2 (south Milan, near Porta Romana)

## üëó Quadrilatero della Moda (Fashion District)

### Worth Visiting Even If Not Shopping? YES.

The four streets forming Milan's fashion quadrilateral:
- **Via Montenapoleone** ‚Äî The main artery. Flagship stores for Valentino, Dior, Gucci, Bulgari, Cartier
- **Via della Spiga** ‚Äî Pedestrianized, elegant. Dolce & Gabbana, Sergio Rossi, Tod's
- **Via Sant'Andrea** ‚Äî Chanel, Armani, Trussardi
- **Via Manzoni** ‚Äî Armani/Silos museum at one end

### Why Visit Without Shopping
- **People watching:** Milan's most stylish humans parade here. It's a fashion show on the street.
- **Architecture:** Beautiful historic palazzi house the stores. Many flagship stores are designed by star architects.
- **Armani/Silos:** Giorgio Armani's personal museum (~‚Ç¨12). Four floors of iconic designs spanning 40+ years. A must for anyone interested in fashion/design.
- **Window displays:** The visual merchandising is an art form. These windows are styled by top creative directors.
- **Free to walk through:** It's a public neighborhood with excellent gelato spots and caf√©s
- **Cova Pasticceria (Via Montenapoleone 8):** Historic pastry shop since 1817. Expensive but worth one coffee.

## üçΩÔ∏è Best Restaurants & Food

### Classic Milanese Dishes to Try
- **Risotto alla Milanese:** Saffron risotto ‚Äî the city's signature dish. Creamy, golden, rich.
- **Cotoletta alla Milanese:** The original breaded veal cutlet (predates Wiener Schnitzel). Bone-in, thick, butter-fried.
- **Ossobuco:** Braised veal shank with gremolata. Often paired with risotto.
- **Mondeghili:** Milanese meatballs (ground leftover boiled meats)
- **Panettone:** Milan's gift to Christmas worldwide. Year-round at good bakeries.
