## üé® Scrovegni Chapel (Cappella degli Scrovegni)

### Why It Matters
The Scrovegni Chapel houses **the single most important fresco cycle in Western art** ‚Äî Giotto's revolutionary masterpiece, painted 1303‚Äì1305. These frescoes essentially **invented modern painting**:
- First true use of perspective and three-dimensional space (a century before the Renaissance)
- Unprecedented emotional realism in human figures
- The famous blue ceiling ‚Äî a mantle of stars you'll never forget
- Scenes include *The Kiss of Joachim and Anne at the Golden Gate*, *The Lamentation of Christ*, *The Last Judgement*

The cycle narrates the History of Salvation: Stories of the Virgin and Christ along the walls, Vices and Virtues in the lower register, and the massive Last Judgement on the entrance wall.

## ‚õ™ Basilica of Saint Anthony (Basilica di Sant'Antonio)

Known locally as "Il Santo" ‚Äî Paduans simply say "the Saint" and everyone knows who they mean. One of the most important pilgrimage sites in Italy.

### Highlights
- **Architecture:** Romanesque-Gothic with Byzantine domes ‚Äî looks almost like a mini-Istanbul skyline
- **Donatello's bronze sculptures:** The high altar features extraordinary bronzes by Donatello (1444‚Äì1449), including the famous *Crucifix* and *Madonna with Child*
- **Chapel of the Saint:** Contains St. Anthony's tomb ‚Äî expect pilgrims touching the marble
- **Relics:** The chapel preserves Anthony's tongue and jawbone (yes, really ‚Äî still intact after 800 years)
- **Cloisters:** Four beautiful cloisters, peaceful and uncrowded

### Practical Info
- **Free entry** (donations welcome)
- **Open daily:** 6:15 AM‚Äì7:45 PM (June)
- **Dress code:** Shoulders and knees covered (wraps available at entrance)
- **Location:** 15 min walk south from city center
- **Time needed:** 45 min‚Äì1 hour

## üèõÔ∏è University of Padua (Universit√† degli Studi di Padova)

### History
- **Founded 1222** ‚Äî second oldest in Italy (after Bologna), among the oldest in the world
- Galileo Galilei taught here for 18 years (1592‚Äì1610) ‚Äî his wooden lectern (cattedra) is preserved
- First university in the world to award a degree to a woman (Elena Lucrezia Cornaro Piscopia, 1678)
- Historic ties to the Venetian Republic, which protected academic freedom

### What to See
- **Palazzo del Bo** (main historic building): Home to the world's first permanent anatomical theatre (Teatro Anatomico, 1594) ‚Äî a stunning oval wooden amphitheatre where cadavers were dissected before 300 students
- **Galileo's lectern** (cattedra di Galileo)
- **Aula Magna** ‚Äî grand hall with walls covered in coats of arms of former students
- **Guided tours available** ‚Äî check the university website; tours run several times daily in Italian and English

### Practical Info
- **Location:** Via VIII Febbraio, right in the city center (across from Caff√® Pedrocchi)
- **Tour price:** ~‚Ç¨7‚Äì10
- **Duration:** ~45 min
- **Tip:** The anatomical theatre alone is worth the visit ‚Äî claustrophobically beautiful

## üåø Botanical Garden (Orto Botanico di Padova)

### Why It's Special
- **Founded 1545** ‚Äî the world's oldest academic botanical garden still in its original location
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site** since 1997
- Originally created to grow medicinal plants for the university's medical faculty
- Home to the "Goethe Palm" ‚Äî a Mediterranean fan palm planted in 1585, which inspired Goethe's essay on plant morphology

### What to See
- **Historic garden:** Circular walled garden with original Renaissance layout, 6,000+ plant species
- **Garden of Biodiversity:** Modern greenhouse complex (opened 2014) simulating biomes from tropical to arid ‚Äî architecturally stunning glass and steel structure
- **Carnivorous plants, orchids, medicinal herbs, aquatic plants**

### Practical Info
- **Address:** Via Orto Botanico, 15 (near Prato della Valle)
- **Hours (June):** 10:00 AM‚Äì7:00 PM (Tue‚ÄìSun), closed Mondays
- **Price:** ~‚Ç¨10 full / ‚Ç¨8 reduced
- **Time needed:** 1‚Äì1.5 hours (more if you love plants)
- **Guided tours** available ‚Äî worth it for a first visit
- **Website:** [ortobotanicopd.it](https://www.ortobotanicopd.it/en/)

## üçπ Aperitivo & Spritz Culture ‚Äî The Birthplace

### The Padua Spritz Story
Padua has a legitimate claim as the **birthplace of the modern Aperol Spritz**. Aperol was created in Padua in 1919 by the Barbieri brothers, who debuted it at the Padua International Fair. The spritz tradition (adding sparkling water to wine) was already Venetian, but the Aperol version became Padua's identity.

GQ once named Padua the **"public drinking capital of the world"** ‚Äî and at 6 PM on any given day, you'll understand why. The entire city floods its piazzas for aperitivo.

## üõí Food Markets

### Piazza delle Erbe & Piazza della Frutta
Padua's **giant open-air market** sprawls across two connected piazzas in the shadow of the medieval Palazzo della Ragione. This is the real deal ‚Äî not a tourist market.

- **Piazza delle Erbe** (herbs/vegetables): Fresh produce, seasonal fruits, vegetables
- **Piazza della Frutta** (fruit): More produce, plus street food stalls
- **Il Salone** (covered arcades under Palazzo della Ragione): Irresistible delicatessens ‚Äî salami, prosciutto, Parmigiano Reggiano, salt cod, fresh pasta, cheese shops
- **Hours:** Mon‚ÄìSat, roughly 7:00 AM‚Äì1:00 PM
- **Saturday** is the biggest market day (also extends to Prato della Valle)

### Palazzo della Ragione
The massive medieval hall that separates the two market piazzas. Built 1218, with the largest unsupported medieval hall roof in Europe. Stunning frescoed interior (astrological cycle). Worth visiting:
- **Hours:** Tue‚ÄìSun 9:00‚Äì19:00, closed Mondays
- **Tickets:** ‚Ç¨8 full / ‚Ç¨6 reduced (+‚Ç¨1 booking fee)

## üèòÔ∏è Other Notable Sights

### Baptistery of the Cathedral
- Houses one of the most important 14th-century fresco cycles: **Giusto de' Menabuoi's masterpiece** (1375‚Äì1376)
- Part of the Urbs Picta UNESCO inscription alongside Scrovegni
- Often overlooked by tourists ‚Äî less crowded, equally stunning
- Small entry fee

### The Ghetto Quarter
- Maze of narrow, arcaded streets off Piazza delle Erbe
- Jewish community segregated here 1603‚Äì1797 (opened by Napoleon)
- Today: antique shops, textile bazaars (Manfrotto, Tessuti Bevilacqua), vinyl record shops (Music Addict), excellent bars and restaurants
- Visit the **Jewish Museum and Synagogue** for history

### Caff√® Pedrocchi
- Beyond just a bar ‚Äî it's a monument. Neoclassical architecture, opened 1831
- Known as "the caf√© without doors" ‚Äî it was open 24/7 for over a century
- Site of the 1848 student uprising against Austrian rule (bullet hole still visible)
- Upper floor: ornate themed rooms (Egyptian, Greek, Roman) ‚Äî small museum fee
