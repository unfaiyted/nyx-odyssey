## üèõÔ∏è Must-See Sites

### St. Mark's Basilica (Basilica di San Marco)
- **Cost:** Free entry (skip-the-line booking ~‚Ç¨6-9 online)
- **Museum/Pala d'Oro:** ‚Ç¨5-7 extra
- **Dress code:** Covered shoulders and knees required
- **Hours:** Mon-Sat 9:30-17:15, Sun 14:00-17:15 (summer until later)
- **Pre-book?** **YES ‚Äî strongly recommended.** Lines can exceed 1-2 hours in summer
- **Tip:** No large bags allowed. Free bag check nearby.
- **Book at:** [basilicasanmarco.it](https://www.basilicasanmarco.it) or [venetoinside.com](https://www.venetoinside.com)

### Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale)
- **Cost:** ‚Ç¨30-35 standard / ‚Ç¨15 reduced (students, seniors, children 6-14)
- **Online (30+ days ahead):** ‚Ç¨30 standard
- **Hours:** Apr-Oct 9:00-19:00 (last entry 18:00) / Nov-Mar 9:00-18:00
- **Includes:** Correr Museum, Archaeological Museum, Monumental Rooms of Biblioteca Marciana
- **Pre-book?** **YES ‚Äî essential in summer.** Book at [palazzoducale.visitmuve.it](https://palazzoducale.visitmuve.it)
- **Highlight:** Bridge of Sighs (visible from inside and outside), grand council chamber

### Rialto Bridge & Market
- **Cost:** Free
- **Market hours:** Fish market Tue-Sat ~7:30-12:00; produce market daily except Sun
- **Tip:** Go early morning for the real experience. By 10 AM it's packed with tourists.

### Murano (Glass Island)
- **How to get there:** Vaporetto Line 3 from Piazzale Roma (~25 min) or Line 4.1/4.2
- **Time needed:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Must-do:** Watch a free glass-blowing demonstration (factories near the vaporetto stops)
- **Museo del Vetro (Glass Museum):** ‚Ç¨12
- **Tip:** Factories offer free demos but will pressure you to buy. No obligation.

### Burano (Colorful Houses)
- **How to get there:** Vaporetto Line 12 from Murano Faro (~30 min) or from Fondamente Nove (~45 min)
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours (including lunch)
- **Must-do:** Walk the colorful streets, photograph Piazza Galuppi, eat fresh seafood
- **Lace Museum:** ‚Ç¨5
- **Tip:** Best island for lunch ‚Äî try Trattoria al Gatto Nero or Riva Rosa

### San Giorgio Maggiore
- **Bell tower:** ‚Ç¨8 ‚Äî panoramic 360¬∞ views of Venice, MUCH less crowded than St. Mark's campanile
- **Church:** Free, beautiful Palladian architecture
- **How:** Vaporetto Line 2 from San Zaccaria (~5 min ride)
- **Pro tip:** This is the best-kept-secret viewpoint in Venice

## üö¢ Vaporetto (Water Bus) Guide

### Ticket Options
| Pass | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Single ride (75 min) | ‚Ç¨7.50-9.50 | One or two rides only |
| 24-hour pass | ‚Ç¨25 | Full day exploring |
| 48-hour pass | ‚Ç¨35 | Two-day trip |
| 72-hour pass | ‚Ç¨45 | Extended stay |
| Rolling Venice (under 29) | ‚Ç¨27/3 days | Youth discount |

### Tips
- **Buy at:** ACTV ticket offices at Santa Lucia station, Piazzale Roma, or online via [veneziaunica.it](https://www.veneziaunica.it)
- **Validate:** Tap card on yellow reader before boarding EVERY time
- **Key lines:**
  - **Line 1:** Slow route down Grand Canal (all stops) ‚Äî great for sightseeing
  - **Line 2:** Fast route down Grand Canal (fewer stops)
  - **Line 3:** Direct to Murano from Piazzale Roma
  - **Line 12:** Murano ‚Üî Burano ‚Üî Torcello
  - **Line 4.1/4.2:** Circular route around Venice + Murano
- **Strategy for 1 day:** If you'll ride 3+ times, the 24-hour pass pays for itself
- **Walking is often faster:** Venice is only ~45 min walk end-to-end. Use vaporetto mainly for Grand Canal, islands, and tired legs.

## ‚òÄÔ∏è Summer-Specific Tips

### Beating the Crowds
- **Arrive by 8:30 AM** ‚Äî Venice is nearly empty before 10 AM
- **Visit St. Mark's at opening (9:30)** or late afternoon
- **Avoid:** 10 AM‚Äì4 PM at major sites (peak cruise ship + day tripper hours)
- **Best strategy:** Do major sites early morning, explore neighborhoods midday, return to popular areas at golden hour
- **Tuesday-Thursday** generally less crowded than weekends
- **Early June** is best compromise ‚Äî warm but not peak insanity yet
- **July-August:** Hottest, most crowded, many locals on holiday

### Heat Management
- **Temperatures:** 28-35¬∞C (82-95¬∞F) in July-August, high humidity
- **Carry water** ‚Äî fountains exist but aren't abundant
- **Midday break:** Take a long lunch or visit air-conditioned museums 12-3 PM
- **Gelato strategy:** Multiple stops throughout the day are medically necessary
- **Shade:** Narrow alleys are naturally cooler than open squares
- **Sun protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses essential. Limited shade on vaporettos.

### Flooding (Acqua Alta)
- **Summer:** Very unlikely. This is a fall/winter phenomenon (Oct-Mar)
- **Not a concern** for summer visits

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Get lost on purpose.** Venice's magic is in the unexpected ‚Äî a quiet campo, a canal with no one around, a hidden church. Put away the phone for an hour.

2. **Spritz protocol:** Aperol Spritz is tourist default. Locals drink Select Spritz (more bitter, more Venetian). Try both.

3. **Cicchetti crawl > sit-down restaurant** for lunch. Hit 3-4 bacari near Rialto between 11-1 PM.

4. **Water:** Tap water is safe and free in Venice (from the mainland aqueduct). Ask for "acqua del rubinetto" at restaurants.

5. **Navigation:** Venice has yellow signs pointing to major landmarks (Rialto, San Marco, Ferrovia/station, Piazzale Roma). Follow these when lost.

6. **Gondola reality check:** ‚Ç¨80 for 30 min (‚Ç¨100 after 7 PM) for up to 6 people. Romantic but expensive. Alternative: ‚Ç¨2 traghetto (gondola ferry) across Grand Canal at several crossings.

7. **No wheels in Venice.** No rolling suitcases if you can avoid it ‚Äî bridges = stairs everywhere.

8. **Scam watch:** Only buy glass marked "Vetro Artistico Murano" with the trademark. Lots of Chinese-made fakes.

9. **Free church art:** Many churches have incredible art. Santa Maria Gloriosa dei Frari and San Zaccaria have masterpieces rivaling any museum.

10. **The best time in Venice is 7-9 PM.** Day trippers have left, cruise ships have departed, golden light hits the canals. This is when Venice becomes Venice.

## üé≠ Hidden Gems & Lesser-Known Experiences

### Libreria Acqua Alta
- A bookshop where books are stored in gondolas, bathtubs, and waterproof bins (because flooding). Cats everywhere. Staircase made of old encyclopedias. Instagram-famous but still charming.
- **Location:** Castello, near Campo Santa Maria Formosa
- **Cost:** Free to browse

### Scala Contarini del Bovolo
- Hidden Renaissance spiral staircase with rooftop views. Most tourists never find it.
- **Location:** Near Campo Manin, San Marco
- **Cost:** ‚Ç¨8
- **Tip:** Follow the small signs from Campo Manin ‚Äî it's tucked in an alley

### Traghetto Rides
- For ‚Ç¨2, ride a gondola across the Grand Canal standing up like a local. Seven traghetto crossings exist. The one near Ca' d'Oro ‚Üí Rialto Market is the most scenic.

### Squero di San Trovaso
- One of the last working gondola boatyards. You can watch craftsmen from across the canal (best viewed from Osteria Al Squero with a spritz in hand).

### Giudecca Island
- Just across from Dorsoduro (Vaporetto Line 2). Almost zero tourists. Visit the Molino Stucky (now a Hilton, but the rooftop bar has incredible views). Walk the Fondamenta to Chiesa del Redentore (Palladio).

### Early Morning Fish Market
- Rialto's Pescheria at 7:30 AM is a sensory explosion ‚Äî fishmongers unloading the catch, locals haggling, no tourists. Pair with coffee at a nearby bacaro.
