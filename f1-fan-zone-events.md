## 1. F1 Fan Zone (Included with Race Ticket)

### What It Is
The official F1 Fan Zone is a large, curated area **inside the circuit grounds** that all ticketholders can access. It's been a highlight of the Las Vegas GP since 2023.

### Location
- Typically set up in the **paddock/infield area near the Las Vegas Strip** between Turns 1-5
- Exact location varies year to year but always within the circuit footprint

### What's Inside (Based on 2023-2025)
- **F1 Merchandise Superstore** — Official team and F1 gear, exclusive Las Vegas GP merchandise
- **Team Fan Zones** — Individual team activations (Red Bull, Ferrari, McLaren, Mercedes, etc.)
- **F1 Simulators** — Race simulators where fans can drive the Las Vegas circuit
- **DJ Stages & Live Music** — Concerts and DJ sets throughout race week
- **Photo Opportunities** — Show cars, trophies, podium replicas
- **F1 Esports Arena** — Competitive gaming stations
- **Food & Beverage Village** — Multiple vendors, bars, and dining options
- **F1 Authentics** — Purchase race-used memorabilia (bodywork, tires, etc.)
- **Autograph Sessions** — Scheduled driver and team personnel appearances

### Cost
- **Included with any race ticket** (GA, grandstand, or hospitality)
- No additional charge to enter the Fan Zone
- Individual activities inside may have separate costs (simulators, premium experiences)

### Hours
- Typically opens 3-4 hours before first on-track session and stays open through the evening
- Thursday: ~2:00 PM - 10:00 PM
- Friday/Saturday: ~2:00 PM - Late (post-session)

## 2. Pit Lane Walk

### What It Is
An exclusive opportunity to walk down the actual pit lane, see the team garages up close, and get within feet of the F1 cars.

### When
- **Thursday of race week** (Nov 19) — typically afternoon, before track goes live
- Sometimes also offered on Friday morning before Practice 1

### Cost & Access
- **Often included with 3-day ticket packages** (check when purchasing)
- Some grandstand/hospitality packages include pit lane walk as a perk
- Separate pit walk tickets may be available: **~$50-100 if sold separately**
- Limited capacity — first-come, first-served or time-slot based

### Tips
- Arrive early for the best experience — it gets crowded
- Bring your camera — photo ops with cars and garage setups
- Dress warm — November nights in Vegas can be chilly (45-50°F)

## 3. Driver Appearances & Autograph Sessions

### Official FIA Drivers' Parade
- **Saturday pre-race** (before the Grand Prix)
- Drivers parade around the circuit on the back of trucks/classic cars
- Free viewing from any ticketed area

### Autograph Sessions
- Typically held in the Fan Zone on **Thursday and Friday**
- Drivers rotate through scheduled 15-30 minute signing windows
- **Free but capacity-limited** — arrive 1-2 hours early for popular drivers
- 2024/2025 had sessions with 4-6 drivers per day

### Media Events & Press Conferences
- FIA press conferences happen Thursday; open to public via big screens in Fan Zone
- Team social media often announces surprise appearances

## 4. F1 Las Vegas Fan Festival / Grand Prix Plaza

### Overview
In 2024 and 2025, the LVGP organizers created a **free public fan festival** in the days leading up to the race, separate from the ticketed circuit area.

### What to Expect
- **Location:** Typically on or near the Las Vegas Strip (Bellagio Fountains area, LINQ Promenade, or similar)
- **FREE to attend** — no race ticket required
- Live entertainment, DJs, and performances
- F1 show car demonstrations
- Team merchandise pop-ups
- Interactive exhibits and photo ops
- Food and drink vendors

### When
- Usually **Wednesday-Thursday** of race week (Nov 18-19)
- Some activities may extend through Saturday

### Cost
- **FREE admission** to the public festival areas
- Food/drink/merchandise at your own cost

## 5. Off-Track F1 Events & Experiences

### Casino & Hotel F1 Takeovers
Las Vegas hotels go all-in during F1 week:
- **Bellagio:** F1-themed fountain shows, lobby displays
- **The Venetian/Palazzo:** Team viewing parties and themed events
- **Wynn/Encore:** Exclusive F1 viewing parties (ticketed, $200-500+)
- **Cosmopolitan:** Rooftop viewing events
- **Fontainebleau:** Premium race viewing packages

### F1 Viewing Parties (For Non-Ticketholders)
- Several Strip hotels and restaurants offer viewing parties with big screens
- Prices range from **free (with food/drink minimum) to $200+ for premium packages**
- Book early — these sell out fast

### F1 Pop-Up Experiences
- **Pirelli Hot Laps** (ticketed, ~$500-1,000) — ride in a supercar driven by a pro around the circuit
- **F1 Paddock Club** (premium hospitality, $5,000+) — includes pit lane access, gourmet dining, celebrity appearances
- **Team Hospitality Suites** — Red Bull, Ferrari, McLaren each host exclusive parties ($2,000-10,000+)

### Las Vegas Strip Activities During F1 Week
- **F1 Car Display at Bellagio** — Show cars on display (free to view)
- **Team Pop-Up Shops** — Along the Strip, temporary stores with exclusive merchandise
- **Sphere F1 Content** — The Sphere typically features F1-themed visuals during race week (visible from outside, free)
- **F1 After-Parties** — Various nightclubs host official and unofficial post-race parties
  - XS Nightclub (Wynn)
  - Hakkasan (MGM Grand)
  - Omnia (Caesars Palace)
  - Expect covers of **$50-200+ during race week**

## 7. Recommendations for Faiyt & Gretchen

### Must-Do (Free/Included)
1. **Fan Zone** — Spend a few hours exploring, hit the simulators, check out team activations
2. **Pit Lane Walk (Thursday)** — Don't miss this if included with your tickets
3. **Fan Festival on the Strip** — Great for Wednesday/Thursday before track sessions begin
4. **Autograph Session** — Pick 1-2 favorite drivers and line up early
5. **Walk the Strip during F1 week** — Hotels go all-out with displays and decorations

### Worth the Splurge
1. **After-Party at a major nightclub** — The energy post-race is electric ($50-150/person)
2. **Viewing party at a nice restaurant** — If you want a break from the grandstands, some upscale restaurants offer great viewing + dinner combos

### Skip
1. Paddock Club / Team Hospitality — Not worth the $5,000+ unless money is no object
2. Pirelli Hot Laps — Cool but pricey for what you get
